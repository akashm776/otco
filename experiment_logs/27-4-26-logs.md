# Question

Does adaptive gated OT-Mix improve over the baseline on CUB-200? And if it does, is the bottleneck the batch-local negative pool — i.e., would a larger, cross-epoch pool give OT better candidates to work with?

---

# Current Understanding

CUB-200 baseline (50 epochs, batch_size=64, SigLIP, both_last_layer) completed:

| Epoch | T→I R@1 | I→T R@1 | Avg R@1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 | 0.81% | 1.38% | 1.10% |
| 40 | 0.81% | 1.12% | 0.97% |
| 50 | **1.05%** | **1.71%** | **1.38%** |

Best = epoch 50 (final), not converged at 50 epochs. Positive cosine similarity never went positive (−0.037 at ep50) — model relied on logit_bias rather than cosine geometry. In-batch recall reached 46.9% (ep42) vs 1.38% pool recall — a 34× gap indicating strong batch-local memorization but poor generalization.

Adaptive gated OT-Mix (`cub200_softmax_mix_adaptive_gated`) completed:

| Epoch | T→I R@1 | I→T R@1 | Avg R@1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| 30 | 0.71% | 1.35% | 1.03% |
| 40 | 0.91% | 1.24% | 1.08% |
| **48** | **1.19%** | **1.69%** | **1.44%** |
| 50 | 1.05% | 1.59% | 1.32% |

Best = epoch 48, **+0.06% over baseline**. The gating worked as intended: `useful` bucket dominant in mature training, `too_easy` and `diffuse` correctly suppressed early and at stale-plan steps. Despite the improvement, T→I was the stronger direction for OT gain (+0.14pp) while I→T was nearly neutral (−0.02pp).

# Gap

The batch-local pool has 64 images per step, minus the positive — effectively 63 OT candidates. CUB-200 has 200 classes; with batch_size=64, the expected number of same-class images per batch is ~0.3 (200 classes × 64/5994 ≈ 2.1 images/class). Most batches contain no same-species candidates. OT can only mix from whatever images happen to be in the batch.

A larger, cross-epoch pool would give OT access to harder, class-relevant candidates without depending on batch composition luck.

---

# Hypothesis

An epoch-start image embedding cache covering all 5994 training images, sampled randomly at N=128 per step (excluding current batch positives), gives OT a meaningfully larger and more class-diverse candidate set. Expected effect: lower `selected_neg_rank_mean` (harder negatives found), sharper plans, and improvement in T→I retrieval.

The pool is detached (no_grad) — gradients flow only through the current batch's image encoder pass (base SigLIP loss) and through the text encoder (synthetic loss). This creates an asymmetry: the synthetic loss trains the text encoder but not the image encoder.

# Executable Test

**New experiment:** `cub200_softmax_mix_cached_pool_128`

Config identical to `cub200_softmax_mix_adaptive_gated` (seed 42, same gating thresholds, same OT hyperparams) with one addition: `pool_size: 128`.

Implementation:
- `_build_image_pool()`: `DataLoader(shuffle=False, drop_last=False)` → forward all training images in eval mode → cache `[5994, 512]` normalized embeddings on device. Runs once per epoch start.
- Per step: build eligible set (exclude positive image_ids), sample `pool_size` without replacement from eligible set.
- `SoftmaxMixLoss` receives `image_pool=[N, d]` tensor; computes OT plan over `[B, N]` logits instead of `[B, B]`.

Key diagnostic to watch: `pool_selected_rank_mean` — expect ≪64 if OT finds harder negatives from the larger pool.

# Structural Update

Adaptive gated OT-Mix is confirmed better than baseline on CUB-200 (+0.06pp). The finding validates the gating hypothesis: per-step conditioning reduces instability and improves final performance.

The residual question is whether the gain is limited by pool quality. Cached-pool experiment launched.
