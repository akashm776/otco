# Research Log

**Date:** 2026-03-11

---

# Objective

Determine whether OT-generated synthetic negatives for a given caption are visually similar to the ground-truth image.

Goal: test whether OT is producing **meaningful hard negatives** or **unrealistic / uninformative negatives**.

---

# Question

For a given caption, do the OT-generated synthetic negatives visually resemble the ground-truth image?

---

# Current Understanding

For OT-generated negatives to be useful, they should behave like **hard negatives**.

That means:

- they should be different from the ground-truth image
- but still visually or semantically similar enough to be informative

If OT-generated negatives are visually unrelated to the ground-truth image, then they are likely not useful hard negatives.

Instead, they may act as noisy or confusing training signals.

---

# Hypothesis

For Flickr8k, OT-generated synthetic negatives for a given caption may not resemble the ground-truth image closely enough to act as meaningful hard negatives.

Possible reason:

- nearest negative images may already be semantically far from the ground-truth image
- OT interpolation between such negatives may produce synthetic negatives that are not realistic semantic neighbors

---

# Experiment

Using a pretrained aligned image encoder **I** and text encoder **T**:

For a given caption:

1. Compute the caption embedding.
2. Retrieve the nearest negative image embeddings.
3. Compute the OT-based synthetic negative embedding.
4. Visualize:
   - the ground-truth image
   - the nearest negative images
   - the OT costs for those negatives
5. Compare whether the nearest negatives appear visually similar to the ground-truth image.

Run details (this log update):

- Output folder analyzed: `outputs/ot_diagnostic`
- Samples evaluated: 100 captions
- Unique GT images: 20 (5 captions per image)
- `top_k=5`, `epsilon=0.07`

---

# Diagnostic Goal

Check whether OT is operating over a neighborhood of negatives that is visually close to the ground-truth image.

If the nearest negatives are already visually dissimilar, then the synthetic OT negative is unlikely to be a meaningful hard negative.

---

# Expected Evidence

If the hypothesis is correct:

- nearest negative images will not strongly resemble the ground-truth image
- OT-generated synthetic negatives will therefore be based on weak or unrelated semantic neighbors
- OT will fail to provide informative hard-negative signal

---

# Decision Criteria

Use these criteria to decide whether OT negatives are robust:

- **Negative outranking rate** `P(neg1 > GT)`:
  - good: < 0.30
  - risky (false-negative pressure): > 0.50
- **Average hardness gap** `E[neg1_sim - gt_sim]`:
  - good: <= 0
  - risky: > 0
- **Synthetic-vs-GT gap** `E[synth_sim - gt_sim]`:
  - good: clearly negative (well below 0)
  - risky: near 0 or positive frequently
- **Geometry sanity** `E[sim(gt, synth)]`:
  - meaningful OT neighborhood: moderately/highly positive (nearby semantics)
  - uninformative OT neighborhood: very low

---

# Structural Update

Observation:

- Quantitative retrieval behavior (100 samples):
  - Recall@1 = 0.35, Recall@5 = 0.79, Recall@10 = 0.89
  - mean GT rank = 7.22, median GT rank = 2
  - long-tail failures exist (max GT rank = 172; 11/100 samples have rank > 10)
- Negative hardness:
  - `fraction_neg1_beats_gt = 0.65` (65/100)
  - `mean_neg1_minus_gt_sim = +0.0254` (top negative is, on average, closer than GT)
- Synthetic negative behavior:
  - `mean_text_gt_sim = 0.0230`
  - `mean_text_synth_sim = 0.0209`
  - `mean(synth_sim - gt_sim) = -0.0021` (almost equal on average)
  - synthetic beats GT in 44/100 samples
- Geometry:
  - `mean_gt_synth_sim = 0.8129` (synthetic negatives remain close to GT in image space)
- Qualitative panels:
  - Many hard negatives are semantically close (examples: dance/market/sports clusters), not random garbage.
  - Worst failures occur when caption-to-GT similarity is weak/negative and nearby alternatives dominate.

Implication:

- OT is usually operating on a **meaningful neighborhood** (not obviously unrealistic negatives).
- Main failure mode is **false-negative pressure**: nearest negatives often outrank GT, so OT may amplify ambiguity.
- This suggests the issue is less "OT builds nonsense negatives" and more "embedding separation is insufficient + dataset has unlabeled near-positives".

Conclusion:

- Initial hypothesis is **not supported as written**.
- OT negatives are often semantically plausible and hard, but frequently too competitive with GT.
- Revised conclusion:
  - OT in this setup is not primarily failing because neighbors are visually dissimilar.
  - It is more likely limited by encoder geometry / label ambiguity, causing robust hard negatives to drift into false negatives.
