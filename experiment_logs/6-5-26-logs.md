# Question

Does N=512 confirm saturation of the random cached-pool approach — i.e., does doubling from N=256 to N=512 degrade Avg R@1 rather than improve it?

---

# Current Understanding

| Setting | T→I R@1 | I→T R@1 | Avg R@1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 1.05% | 1.71% | 1.38% |
| Cached-pool N=128 detached | **1.36%** | 1.61% | 1.48% |
| Cached-pool N=256 detached | 1.26% | **1.73%** | **1.49%** |

N=256 is the marginal best (1.49%) but with diffuse-dominant gate (64% of ep30+ steps suppressed). Hypothesis: N=512 increases diffuse dominance further and Avg drops below N=256.

# Structural Update

**Final Phase III comparison:**

| Setting | T→I R@1 | I→T R@1 | Avg R@1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline (ep50) | 1.05% | 1.71% | 1.38% |
| Adaptive gated (ep48) | 1.19% | 1.69% | 1.44% |
| Live re-forward N=128 (ep40) | 1.17% | **1.71%** | 1.44% |
| Cached-pool N=512 (ep36, partial) | 1.04% | 1.45% | 1.24% |
| Cached-pool N=128 detached (ep49) | **1.36%** | 1.61% | 1.48% |
| Cached-pool N=256 detached (ep47) | 1.26% | 1.73% | **1.49%** |

The N=512 result at ep36 (1.24%) is below N=256 at the same epoch stage (ep35: 1.13% → best ep47: 1.49%), consistent with saturation. N=512 appears to either plateau earlier or is degraded by the larger pool's random dilution. The run is partial — a full 50-epoch run might recover, but the trajectory at ep36 trails N=256 by ~0.25pp at the same training stage.

**Conclusion:** Random cached-pool OT-Mix peaks around N=128–256. The ceiling is approximately 1.48–1.49% Avg R@1 on this CUB-200 setting. Larger pools shift the T→I/I→T balance toward I→T (consistent with detached asymmetry + harder pool negatives dominating I→T) but do not improve the overall average. N=512 provides the saturation confirmation: the approach does not scale monotonically with pool size under random sampling.

# Next Question

The random pool ceiling is ~1.49%. The gap between OT-Mix and baseline is real but modest (+0.11pp on a 1.38% base). Is the bottleneck the random pool construction (structure-blind sampling), or the CUB-200 task difficulty (200 classes, ~30 images/class, 5794 val images)?

Two candidate directions:
1. **Class-stratified pool**: instead of random sampling from all 5994 embeddings, stratify by predicted pseudo-class to guarantee near-species candidates in every step. May require a lightweight cluster assignment or class label injection.
2. **Paper writeup**: current results are sufficient to report. Phase III (cached pool) is the best method overall and the directional asymmetry finding is a clean negative result that bounds the contribution of each encoder to the OT objective.
