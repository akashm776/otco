# Question

If encoder geometry is better separated, does OT keep its hard-negative benefits while reducing false-negative pressure?

---

# Current Understanding

From the March 11 diagnostic (100 captions, 20 unique images, `top_k=5`, `epsilon=0.07`):

- OT negatives are **semantically plausible** — neighbors are meaningful, not garbage.
- The main failure mode is **false-negative pressure**:
  - `P(neg1 > GT) = 0.65` — top negative outranked GT in 65/100 samples
  - `E[neg1_sim - gt_sim] = +0.0254` — top negative is on average *closer* to the caption than GT
  - `P(synth > GT) = 0.44` — synthetic beats GT in nearly half of samples
- GT-to-synthetic image similarity is high: `mean(sim(gt, synth)) = 0.8129` — OT operates in a meaningful neighborhood.
- Root cause hypothesis: encoder geometry is not well separated. GT is not consistently the nearest image to its caption.

---

# Gap

We do not yet know whether this false-negative pressure is caused by:

1. **Insufficient encoder training** — the model hasn't converged enough for GT to reliably rank above hard negatives.
2. **Label ambiguity intrinsic to Flickr8k** — many near-positives exist that are genuinely semantically close to the caption but not labeled as GT.
3. **Both**, meaning no amount of training fixes (2) without multi-positive masking.

---

# Hypothesis

Better encoder separation (from a more fully trained checkpoint) will reduce `P(neg1 > GT)` and `E[neg1_sim - gt_sim]`, but the improvement will plateau or remain noisy due to Flickr8k's unlabeled near-positives. The synth-vs-GT gap will widen, making OT negatives less risky as false-negatives.
