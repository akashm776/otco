# Question

Is OT-Mix's +0.40% gain on Flickr30K real, or explained by extra training? And if it is extra training, why doesn't OT-Mix add signal on this dataset?

---

# Current Understanding

OT-Mix α=0.05 fine-tune (30 epochs from baseline best_model.pt) peaked at 32.50% (epoch 28) — +0.40% over baseline's 32.10%.

The comparison is confounded: OT-Mix starts from the same converged checkpoint and trains for 30 additional epochs. Any of three things could explain the gain: (1) OT auxiliary loss, (2) extra gradient steps, (3) different LR dynamics during fine-tuning.

Additionally: adaptive entropy-based OT warmup was implemented to replace fixed `warmup_steps` for scratch runs. Rationale: with random embeddings, OT plans are uniform/meaningless — `warmup_steps` hardcodes a fixed delay, but the right trigger is "when are plans non-degenerate?" Trigger criterion: coupling_entropy < 3.0 (observed uniform ceiling ≈ 3.33; healthy operating range ≈ 2.0–2.5).

A scratch run was also needed as a clean comparison for the paper (same total epochs, different loss only).

---

# Gap

No controlled ablation existed to separate OT signal from extra training in the fine-tune setting. Without a `continued_baseline` (SigLIP only, same start, same 30 epochs), the +0.40% claim is not defensible.

For scratch runs: fixed `warmup_steps` (e.g., 9000) would delay OT regardless of embedding geometry. Early in training, logit_bias ≈ 0 and scale ≈ small → most synthetics pass gate (gate_sim=−4.0 << 0) even though plans are meaningless.

---

# Hypothesis

Continued baseline will also beat 32.10% — demonstrating the gain is from extra training, not OT. Predicted: continued baseline reaches 32.3–32.5% within 20–25 epochs.

Adaptive warmup prediction: OT triggers at ≈ epoch 24–25 of scratch run (when embedding geometry is sufficiently developed). This matches observed training log (at epoch 27, alpha was already at full 0.05).

# Structural Update

**CRITICAL FINDING: OT-Mix fine-tune result is null.**

The +0.40% (32.10% → 32.50%) is entirely explained by extra gradient steps on the converged model. SigLIP alone, given the same starting point, achieves the same result faster.

**Full Flickr30K picture:**

| Experiment | Loss | Start | Epochs | Best R@1 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | SigLIP | scratch | 50 | 32.10% |
| OT-Mix fine-tune (α=0.05) | SigLIP + OT | baseline ckpt | 30 | 32.50% |
| Continued baseline | SigLIP | baseline ckpt | 30 | 32.50% (ep17) |
| OT-Mix scratch | SigLIP + OT | scratch | 50 | 31.25% |

OT-Mix from scratch is −0.85% WORSE than baseline from scratch.

**Root cause diagnosis (from literature review of 7 papers):**
Flickr30K captions are generic high-level descriptions. Random in-batch negatives are already semantically distant → OT finds no meaningful hard-negative structure → barycentric negatives are uninformative noise at the same scale as the SigLIP loss. OT does not hurt (α is small), but it adds no signal.

Papers confirming this interpretation:
- "Enhancing Multimodal Learning with Hard Negatives" (2403.02875): even 1.5% hard negatives by keyword substitution yields +7–30pp on fine-grained retrieval — standard random negatives in image-text retrieval are too easy
- SynCo (2410.02401): synthetic negatives must be calibrated for difficulty; OT's Sinkhorn plan has no difficulty calibration
- Cluster Contrast: negatives within semantic clusters are more informative than global batch negatives
