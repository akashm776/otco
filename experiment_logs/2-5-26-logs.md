OT diagnostic summary (ep30–50):

- `coupling_entropy`: 2.87–3.06, same range as N=128
- Gap bucket distribution (ep30+): useful=14, diffuse=25 — **diffuse dominant**

vs N=128 (ep30–50): useful=63, diffuse=21, too_easy=4, too_hard=2

**Directional shift:**
- T→I: 1.26% vs N=128's 1.36% — degraded

- Avg: 1.49% vs N=128's 1.48% — effectively flat

The directional shift mirrors the live re-forward result: more candidates → OT selects harder negatives that are more relevant for I→T → text gains less relative to image in T→I direction. This is consistent with the detached pool asymmetry but the diffuse dominance (25 vs 14 useful steps) confirms the larger pool is spreading OT plan mass, forcing the gate to suppress more steps.

# Structural Update

**Updated comparison table:**

| Setting | T→I R@1 | I→T R@1 | Avg R@1 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Baseline | 1.05% | 1.71% | 1.38% |
| Adaptive gated | 1.19% | 1.69% | 1.44% |
| Cached-pool N=128 detached | **1.36%** | 1.61% | 1.48% |
| Live re-forward N=128 | 1.17% | **1.71%** | 1.44% |
| Cached-pool N=256 detached | 1.26% | 1.73% | **1.49%** |

N=256 achieves a marginal improvement in Avg R@1 (1.49% vs 1.48%) driven by I→T, not T→I. The diffuse suppression growing to 25/39 steps (64%) in mature training indicates that half the OT steps in the larger pool are already hitting the entropy ceiling. The pattern is consistent with random pool sampling having a quality ceiling: beyond a certain N, new candidates are drawn from distant classes rather than near-species, making the OT plan more uniform and thus more frequently suppressed.

**Interpretation:** N=256 shifts the T→I/I→T tradeoff rather than strictly improving both. The Avg improvement is real but marginal. The gate is working as intended — diffuse plans are correctly suppressed — but the suppression frequency itself reflects reduced effective pool quality per step.
