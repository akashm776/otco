# Research Log

**Date:** 2026-04-23

# Question

Does α=0.1 over-destabilize the converged SigLIP baseline when fine-tuning with logit-space OT-Mix? And if so, what α is appropriate?

---

# Current Understanding

Logit-space OT-Mix produces sharp, meaningful transport plans (coupling_entropy ≈ 2.0–2.5, peak_mass ≈ 0.40–0.43). The OT auxiliary loss is geometrically correct.

However, the model starts from a fully converged baseline (epoch 48, 32.10%). The OT loss with α=0.1 applies to 5–33 active synthetics per batch (gate_sim=−4.0 filters the rest). The epoch 2 dip in the α=0.1 run was −3.15%:
- Epoch 1: 30.20%
- Epoch 2: 27.05% (dip)
- Epoch 9 (peak): 30.80%

Peak never exceeded baseline (32.10%). Model oscillated 29–30% after epoch 9 and never recovered.

---

# Gap

α=0.1 introduces OT loss contribution that is 10% of total loss at full ramp. For a converged model, this is a large perturbation. The epoch 2 dip of −3.15% suggests the OT loss is fighting against the established embedding geometry before the model can adapt.

The α ramp reaches full strength by step 1000 (≈ 2.2 epochs from start). The epoch 2 dip happens exactly when α approaches its maximum — the model hasn't had time to adapt.

---

# Hypothesis

Halving α to 0.05 will:
1. Reduce the epoch 2 dip from −3.15% to ≈ −1.5% (proportional to α)
2. Allow the model to retain more of its baseline embedding geometry during adaptation
3. Eventually reach a higher peak than α=0.1

The oscillation pattern (∼5-epoch cycles with rising peaks) is inherent to OT fine-tuning — it won't disappear with lower α, but peak heights should rise further.

---

# Executable Test

Create `softmax_mix_k32_logit_a05_from_baseline`: identical to α=0.1 config except `alpha: 0.05`. Run 30 epochs from same baseline checkpoint.

Track:
- Epoch 2 dip magnitude
- Peak R@1 trajectory across 30 epochs
- Whether any peak exceeds 32.10% (baseline)

- Epoch 2 dip: −1.45% (vs −3.15% with α=0.1) — confirmed proportional
- Best: epoch 28, **32.50%** → beats baseline by +0.40%
- Oscillation pattern confirmed: ∼5-epoch cycles with rising peaks
- Experiment logged: `experiments/exp_20260424_050925.json`

α=0.1 comparison:
- Epoch 2 dip: −3.15%
- Peak: 30.80% (epoch 9), never beat baseline

# Structural Update

α=0.05 successfully beats the baseline: **32.50% vs 32.10% (+0.40%)**. Lower α reduces initial destabilization enough for the model to adapt while still providing OT signal.

**New configs created:**
- `softmax_mix_k32_logit_a05_from_baseline` in `experiments.yaml`
- `configs/hf_flickr30k_softmax_mix_k32_logit_a05_from_baseline.yaml`
- `colabs/run_flickr30k_softmax_mix_k32_logit_a05_from_baseline.py`

**Oscillation pattern characterization:**
- ∼5-epoch cycles, peaks at ep5 (31.35%), ep10 (31.50%), ep14 (31.85%), ep27 (31.95%), ep28 (32.50%)
- Peak increments: +0.15, +0.35, +0.10, +0.55 — rising trend
- Deep troughs between peaks (e.g., 29.20% at epoch 19) are inherent, not degradation
